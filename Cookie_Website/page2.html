Chocolate Chip Cookie

Recipe by Panelinha

Go Back 🍪

INGREDIENTS

    7 oz (about 1 ½ cups) of 70% dark chocolate chips
    3.5 oz (7 tablespoons) of cold unsalted butter, cubed
    ½ cup packed brown sugar
    ⅓ cup granulated sugar
    1¼ cups all-purpose flour
    1 teaspoon baking powder
    ½ teaspoon baking soda
    1 pinch of salt
    1 large egg

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. In a bowl, mix together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. In a small bowl, crack the egg and set aside.
  2. Place the cold, cubed butter in a stand mixer and beat with the paddle attachment for about 20 seconds just to soften it slightly.
  3. Add both sugars and beat for another 30 seconds until it forms a moist, crumbly mixture. Add the chocolate chips and mix briefly just to combine.
  4. Add the flour mixture and beat for a few seconds until it resembles a dry crumb. Then add the egg and beat for about 1 minute, just until the dough is fully combined and no dry flour remains. Don’t overmix — you want to avoid developing too much gluten, which would make the cookies dense.
  5. To shape the cookies: measure ¼ cup of dough per cookie. Press and level it in the measuring cup for consistency. Roll into balls with your hands and flatten slightly into disks about 2¾ inches (7 cm) in diameter. Place on a large baking sheet, leaving about ¾ inch (2 cm) between each.
  6. Freeze the baking sheet with the cookie dough for at least 1½ hours. This helps create cookies that are crisp on the outside and soft on the inside.
  7. About 30 minutes before baking, preheat your oven to 350°F (180°C).
  8. Bake for about 15 minutes. The cookies will puff up and turn lightly golden at the edges but stay soft in the center — they’ll crisp up as they cool.
  9. Remove from the oven and let them sit for 15 minutes before eating. This resting time ensures the perfect texture: crispy edges and a gooey center.
